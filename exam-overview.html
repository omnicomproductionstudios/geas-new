The Exam

Exam Overview

A precise, online assessment of real-world English. Proficia measures how effectively you read, listen, write, and speak in authentic contexts—and reports results on the CEFR scale for clarity and portability.

What it measures CEFR alignment

At glance

  • Skills: Reading, Listening, Writing, Speaking
  • Duration: ~90–120 minutes
  • Reporting: CEFR A1–C2
  • Results: within 7 working days

Purpose

What the exam provides

Proficia certifies practical communication. Tasks are designed around purpose and audience—emails, briefings, short reports, conversations—so outcomes reflect how English is used in universities and workplaces, not memorised rules.

  • Scenario-based items — concise prompts that test comprehension, clarity, and intent.
  • Actionable results — CEFR-mapped reporting (A1–C2) that decision-makers understand instantly.
Scope

Where it applies

  • Admissions and placement
  • Hiring and promotion
  • Professional licensing and compliance
  • Personal and global mobility

What it measures

Four core skills assessed through concise, scenario-based tasks. Each task targets purpose, audience, and clarity so performance reflects real-life use of English.

Reading

Identify main ideas, supporting detail, and implied meaning in applied texts and reports.

Listening

Understand multi-accent audio such as briefings, interviews, and short lectures.

Writing

Produce clear emails, summaries, and short analyses with appropriate tone and structure.

Speaking

Respond to interview-style prompts; assessed for coherence, range, and control.

Who it serves

Candidates seeking admission, employment, promotion, or mobility—and organisations that require clear, portable evidence of English ability.

  • Single sitting — ~90–120 minutes.
  • Fast results — within 14 calendar days.
  • Verifiable — digital certificate with unique ID.
  • Accessible — 100% online with live proctoring.
Integrity

Secure, consistent assessment

Identity verification, environment checks, and calibrated scoring methods help ensure fair, repeatable decisions across sessions.

Standards

CEFR alignment

Results are reported on the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR), an international scale from A1 to C2. This lets institutions and employers interpret outcomes at a glance and compare across contexts.

A1

Level A1

Understands and uses everyday expressions; simple interactions with support.

A2

Level A2

Handles routine tasks; can describe basic aspects of background and needs.

B1

Level B1

Deals with familiar matters; produces simple connected text.

B2

Level B2

Understands main ideas of complex text; interacts with fluency and spontaneity.

C1

Level C1

Uses language flexibly for social, academic, and professional purposes.

C2

Level C2

Understands virtually everything; expresses with precision and nuance.
