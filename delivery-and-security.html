Purpose

Why security matters

Delivery and security are designed together: verified identity, monitored sessions, and tamper‑resistant workflows ensure fair outcomes. Institutions receive results they can trust; candidates receive a level that reflects their ability.

  • Integrity by design — layered checks at login, during tasks, and at submission.
  • Global reliability — consistent standards across time zones and regions.
Scope

Where it applies

  • Admissions and placement
  • Hiring and promotion
  • Licensing and compliance
  • Personal and global mobility

A seamless, secure online experience with clear instructions at every step.

  • Global access — take it from anywhere.

  • Privacy — data encrypted in transit and at rest.
  • Clarity — CEFR results in 14 calendar days.

For institutions & employers

Trusted, verifiable outcomes backed by audit trails and policy enforcement.

  • Shareable verification link and certificate ID

How delivery & security work

  • Device & environment checks Camera, microphone, bandwidth, and lighting verified before launch.
  • Identity & liveness Document scan, face match, and real‑time liveness prompts validated by a proctor.
  • Session monitoring Live proctoring plus automated signals: multiple‑face, gaze, secondary display, and noise events.
  • Secure testing window Locked interface with clipboard, print, and navigation restrictions as permitted by OS/browser.
  • Audit & submission Encrypted upload, integrity review flags, and signed submission receipt.
Controls

Integrity controls

Live proctoring

Real‑time human oversight with escalation paths for suspected violations.

Biometric checks

Face match and liveness prompts secure the session against proxy testing.

Secure browser

Restricted clipboard/print/navigation with tamper detection where supported.

Device hygiene

Secondary‑display and window‑switch detection; headphones prompt for Listening.

Encrypted transport

TLS 1.2+ in transit; encrypted storage with role‑based access and audit trails.

Policy enforcement

Clear candidate agreement; progressive responses from warnings to invalidation.

Assurance

Compliance & data protection

  • Data minimisation — collect only what’s required to deliver the exam and verify results.
  • Retention policy — session media stored for a limited period for quality and dispute handling.
  • Access control — least‑privilege access, credential rotation, and audit logs.
  • Regional handling — data centres and safeguards aligned to regional requirements where applicable.
Transparency

Candidate notices

  • Clear consent and policy links at check‑in
  • Proctor chat and escalation routes
  • Access to certificate verification details
