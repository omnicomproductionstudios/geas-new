The four sections

Each section targets how English is used in study and work. Tasks are concise, scenario‑based, and calibrated to CEFR descriptors.

Reading

Identify main ideas, detail, stance, and inference in applied texts: policies, emails, briefs.

  • Item types: multiple‑choice, matching, short response
  • Texts: 250–600 words

Listening

Understand multi‑accent audio: meetings, briefings, interviews, short lectures.

  • Item types: MCQ, note‑fill, ordering
  • Audio: 45–120 seconds each

Writing

Produce clear emails, summaries, and short analyses with appropriate tone and structure.

  • Tasks: short email (100–150 words), response/summary (150–220 words)
  • Scoring: task fulfilment, organisation, range & control

Speaking

Respond to interview‑style prompts; measured for coherence, range, accuracy, and delivery.

  • Prompts: introduction, situational, opinion
  • Recording: 30–90 seconds per prompt

Time guidance & item counts

Section Typical items Suggested time Notes
Reading 20–26 items 25–35 mins Skim first; prioritise high‑value questions.
Listening 18–24 items 20–30 mins Audio plays once; note key nouns/verbs.
Writing 2 tasks 25–35 mins Plan 2–3 mins; review before submit.
Speaking 3–5 prompts 10–15 mins Think aloud briefly; stay on topic.
Flow

Guided sitting

01

System check & check‑in

Verify ID, perform environment scan, and confirm audio/video before the exam opens.

02

Reading → Listening

Begin with comprehension tasks to establish context; instructions appear before each section.

03

Writing

Two tasks: a concise email and a brief response/summary. Word counters and timers are visible.

04

Speaking

Record short responses to prompts. A warm‑up example is provided before scoring starts.

05

Submit & next steps

Confirm submission. Results are issued within 14 calendar days with CEFR reporting.

Preparation

Pacing tips

Use on‑screen timers

Each section shows elapsed/remaining time; move on if stuck and return when possible.

Plan, then write

Spend 2–3 minutes outlining key points before drafting; leave 1–2 minutes to review.

Speak clearly

Answer the question directly, keep sentences concise, and maintain a steady pace.
